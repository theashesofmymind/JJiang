As a conceptual artist, I use work to explore the future of the human body, particularly with impending technological advancement. My work manifests through site-specific performances and performative sculptures. They are my material for exploring how to co-exist in a constantly changing environment.

Growing up with incessant family travels, art helps me adapt to various contexts. I employ instruction, artifacts, and (human) reaction as 3 components of a cyclical model to approach the complexities of Anthropocene existence and their interconnected relationships. Instruction is the rise of a desire in the form of language. Artifact emerges as physical visualizations resulting from instructions, setting the physical limitation for reaction. Reaction unfolds in real-time movements, constantly evaluating the sense of time and rationalizing artifacts into a specific order juggled with culture and history. This model as an imagining tool helps me reconsider the order imposed on the shared reality, conditioned language, and existing knowledge categorizations.

‘A North Pole Iceberg’ is a performance that embodies this approach. ‘A 200m long, 80m tall iceberg’ as described is shown as a piece of holdable ice in a pacifier shape after a series of instructions. Eventually, the performer ate the ice and spit up a piece of bone. Also, I make sculptures to explore how human relate their bodies to artifacts, by visualizing gravity, scale, materiality, and decay on artifacts at site-specific places. I cast a flesh-tone silicone apple filled with expired pills and affixed it to a fruit tree by an arrow in the forest. I used a bedding set to make a Band-Aid with a scar on it, placing it in 8 libraries to catch visitor’s responses. My sculptures provide the opportunity to peek at the hidden side of reality.

Looking forward, I envision further research on the history of narrative technology and museum archives. I aim to investigate more about the nature of creativity and how will it be embodied in the future human body. I consider my work to serve as a critical reflection and creative speculation on the possibilities that lie ahead.

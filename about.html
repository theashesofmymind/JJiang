Being human means navigating through approximately 35,000 conscious decisions daily. As an international learner, I reflect on the multitude of choices that shape not only my path but our collective futures. In an era on the brink of superintelligence, humanity is inextricably intertwined. As an artist, I explore the intricate relationship between the individual’s mentality and the settled reality. I synthesize them by creating undefinable objects, playing the structure of games, and constructing novel scenes at specific sites. My works are research-based, focusing on human agency, they manifest through site-specific sculpture and performances.


Inspired by Stefan Zweig’s Decisive Moments in History, I delve into the fragile yet monumental mentalities of individuals who altered the course of human history. My work reimagines the hidden realities. I use art as a method to recuperate human lost sensations from unchosen choices, reckoning with their long-term impact on life cycles. My process begins with making charts to deconstruct a fragment of reality: history layers, material properties, and how humans perceive it. Through making, I recompose some elements in the chart to highlight moments of vulnerability, ephemerality, and creativity.


Each project is carried out according to the order of the space. In an office setting, I transformed functional objects into a collaborative melancholic narration. At the SAIC gallery, I assembled a collection of forbidden objects and installed them at the entrance throughout the exhibition, inviting viewers to confront what is invisible, censored, or overlooked. To investigate the nature of the invisibles within human perception, I visualize the trace of gravity, randomicity, and entropy on things. I utilized my palm pattern as the surface and path-decider on a coin drop machine structure. During reimagining, I draw project diagrams with the presumption that everything can be given. Later, I reproduced them on aluminum boards to spread them in public spaces—free from the limitations of time, scale, technology, and capital.
